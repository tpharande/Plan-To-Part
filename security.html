Two Factor Authentication

By using Two Factor Authentication, whenever you sign into your Plan To Part account from a new devices,
we will send a code to your phone to verify your infomation as part of your login.
This extra step of security makes sure that no one can steal your password and access to your account.

Securing Your Data

All your data is stored and encrypt with a uniquely derived key for each user. We encrypt every single
pesonally indentifiable field in the database.
The strong password and don't share it with anyone will be a safety way for your information.
This extra step of security makes sure that no one can steal your password and access to your account.

Securing Your Data in Transit

All communications between your and Plan To Part are encrypted. Even if someone eaverdrops on
your communication, they won't be able to decrypt the data in the event that our key is compromised.

Security Questions



What does "bank level security" mean?


Bank-level security means that your important personal information is encrypted and protected using the same industry leading technology that banks use.


Who will have access to my sensitive personal information?


Only the user or the one additional person the user has designated will be able to have access to the sensitive personal information. Plan to Part does not share any of your personal information with outside sources


What happens to my information if I want to delete my Plan To Part account?


You should notify us 30 days in advance and print out any reports before you request to have your account deleted . Plan to Part does not keep any information from a deleted or closed account.


What happens to my personal information if I die?


You will be ask to specify who you want to be notified and have access to your account when you die. All your instructions will be executed as you have requested.


What's two-step verification all about?


Two-step verification is an authentication process that requires two different authentication factors to establish identity. In a nutshell, it means requiring a user to prove their identity in two different ways before granting them access.


How does two-step verification work?


Two-step verification is a process that involves two authentication methods performed one after the other to verity that someone or something requesting access is who or what they are declared to be.
